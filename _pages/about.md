## Research

My mini-undergraduate (6 week) projects have been supervised within the School of Chemistry at Leicester.

- Supervised by [Prof. Shengfu Yang](https://le.ac.uk/people/shengfu-yang). I used DFT and MP2 methods to model hydrogen-bonded molecular complexes, studying intermolecular forces relevant to atmospheric chemistry. I applied counterpoise correction (BSSE) to achieve quantitative agreement with experimental benchmarks.

- Supervised by [Dr. Philip Ash](https://le.ac.uk/people/philip-ash). I synthesised nitrogen-doped carbon quantum dots via microwave-assisted synthesis, characterised their surface chemistry by FTIR and UV-Vis spectroscopy, and analysed their bioconjugation potential through coupling with myoglobin and photoluminescence analysis.

## Interests

Most of my research so far has centred on understanding how molecules interact at a qquantum mechanical level, particularly the weak, non-covalent forces that are easy to overlook but surprisingly consequential for structure and reactivity. I find myself most interested in the gap between what theory can rigorously say and what actually happens in real chemical systems, whether that's something as subtle as an atmospheric hydrogen bond or as tangible as a carbon nanomaterial. Longer term, I'm curious about quantum computing as a route to making currently intractable calculations feasible, and about pushing these methods toward problems in chemistry and broader scientifc problems of the future.
